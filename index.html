Kobe Bryant

'Black Mamba'

Here's a timeline of Kobe Bryant's life:

  • 1978- Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
  • 1981- Starts playing basketball
  • 1984- Bryant's father retires from the NBA and together with his family, moves to Reiti in Italy
  • 1991- Bryant enrolls at Bala Cynwyd Middle school after moving back to Philadelphia and persistently starts his spectacular high-school career by playing on a varsity basketball team as a freshman
  • 1995- Earns the 1995 senior MVP award at Adidas ABCD Camp
  • 1996- Signs a $3.5 million three-year rookie contract with the Lakers making Bryant the first guard drafted directly out of high school
  • 1997- Participates in the Rookie Challenge of the Slam Dunk Contest and clinches the title in the second round with a near-perfect score of 49
  • 1999- Secures three-peat (first three of his eventual five championships) over the Indiana Pacers in 2000, the Philadelphia 76ers in 2001, and the New Jersey Nets in 2002
  • 2006- Scores a career-high 81 points against the Raptors — the second-highest points total in a single game in league history
  • 2008- Joins the United States Men's Basketball team for the 2008 Summer Olympics and scores 13 points in the gold-medal win against Spain
  • 2009- Lakers wins their 15th and 16th (Bryant's fourth and fifth) titles in 2009 and 2010 consecutively
  • 2012- Wins his final gold medal at the 2012 Summer Olympics in London together with the national team
  • 2016- Pens and publishes a poem titled "Dear Basketball" on The Players Tribune to announce his retirement
  • 2018- Wins the Oscar at the 90th Academy Awards for the short film he made out of his "Dear Basketball" poem
  • 2020- Dies at the age of 41

“Have a good time, enjoy life. Life is too short to get bogged down or be discouraged. You have to keep moving, you have to keep going. Put one foot in front of the other and keep on rolling.”-Kobe Bryant, 2008
